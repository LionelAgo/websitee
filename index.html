About Me

Fluid Mechanics Researcher with Expertise in Turbulence Modeling

I am a researcher in fluid mechanics with over 15 years of experience in the study and modeling of wall-bounded turbulent flows. My expertise is built on an interdisciplinary approach that combines fundamental theory of turbulent flows, high-fidelity numerical code development, and implementation of advanced machine learning methods.

Currently based at the Pprime Institute at the University of Poitiers, I lead research on multi-scale interactions in wall-bounded flows, focusing on both fundamental physics and practical applications in drag reduction and heat transfer enhancement.

My work on drag reduction through wall oscillations has significantly contributed to understanding the underlying mechanisms of flow control, demonstrating how coherent turbulent structures can be manipulated to optimize hydrodynamic performance. More recently, I've extended this expertise to the critical domain of heat transfer, establishing non-trivial relationships between heat and momentum transfer mechanisms.

Research Interests

Wall-bounded Turbulence

Investigating multi-scale interactions in turbulent flows, with a focus on the modulation of near-wall turbulence by large-scale outer structures. My work has led to new methods for scale separation and analysis of turbulent fields.

Learn More

Drag Reduction & Heat Transfer

Exploring innovative techniques for flow control using spanwise wall oscillations, with recent breakthroughs in preferential enhancement of heat transfer over drag. My research has demonstrated the breaking of the Reynolds analogy.

Learn More

Machine Learning in Fluid Dynamics

Developing advanced machine learning techniques, particularly auto-encoders, to extract coherent structures from turbulent flow fields and create reduced-order models for prediction and control of complex flow systems.
