Projects Portfolio

My research portfolio includes several funded projects focusing on turbulence modeling, flow control, and machine learning applications in fluid dynamics. These projects span from fundamental research on wall-bounded turbulence to applied studies of heat transfer enhancement in engineering systems.

Through collaborations with academic and industrial partners, these projects aim to advance our understanding of complex flow phenomena and develop innovative methodologies for flow prediction and control.

ANR JCJC INFERENCE

Modeling near-wall turbulence at high Reynolds numbers

The INFERENCE project aims to deepen our understanding of near-wall turbulence at high Reynolds numbers, focusing on the effects of outer flow structures on wall shear and heat transfer. By combining numerical simulations and data-driven methods, this project seeks to develop accurate predictive models for turbulent flows in industrial and environmental applications.

Elucidating mixing mechanisms driving drag and heat transfer
Developing a predictive model for outer structure effects on near-wall turbulence
Building an estimator of near-wall fluctuations from outer flow sensors
Understanding and modeling outer structure effects on controlled near-wall turbulence
Role: Project Lead
Funding: 330,000€
Team: 1 PhD Student + 2 Years Postdoc

2022-2026

ANR SOLAIRE

Machine learning for high-temperature solar receivers

The SOLAIRE project aims to improve the efficiency of concentrated solar power (CSP) plants by focusing on heat transfer optimization in the solar receiver. This initiative is developing active control strategies based on spanwise wall oscillations to enhance heat transfer efficiency while minimizing drag penalties, contributing to the advancement of renewable energy technologies.

Studying the impact of wall oscillations on heat transfer in high-temperature turbulent flows
Developing reinforcement learning algorithms for control parameter optimization
Characterizing the coupling/decoupling between heat transfer and drag under different conditions
Demonstrated for the first time preferential enhancement of heat transfer over drag, breaking the Reynolds analogy
Role: Co-responsible for Pprime team
Funding for Pprime: 179,000€
Partners: PROMES (lead), LISN, Pprime

ANR MUFDD

Data-driven modeling of urban canopy flows

The MUFDD project focuses on developing reduced-order models (ROMs) for urban canopy flows using data-driven approaches. By combining experimental data, numerical simulations, and advanced machine learning techniques, this research aims to provide accurate and computationally efficient tools for modeling complex urban airflows, with applications in urban planning, pollution dispersion, and microclimate analysis.

Developing methods to build estimators and reduced-order models from simulation and experimental data
Integrating physical knowledge in machine learning algorithm design to improve robustness and interpretability
Implementing advanced data assimilation techniques to maintain model accuracy in real-world conditions
Role: Team Member
Funding for Pprime: 182,000€
Partners: LHEEA (lead), IMFT, Pprime

2023-2025

SAFRAN Aircraft Engines Collaboration

Active control of secondary flow instabilities for enhanced heat transfer

This industrial partnership focuses on exploiting secondary flow instabilities to generate longitudinal vortices that enhance heat transfer in aircraft engine cooling ducts. The project leverages Görtler instabilities naturally present on concave walls, with plasma actuators used to optimize vortex characteristics for maximum heat transfer with minimal drag penalty.

Exploiting Görtler instabilities on concave walls for enhanced heat transfer
Optimizing plasma actuator placement and operation for instability control
Developing adaptive control strategies to maximize heat transfer while minimizing pressure losses
Filed a SOLEAU envelope describing the active control concept with the French National Institute of Industrial Property (INPI) on 23/01/2024 (No. DSO2024001272)
Role: Project Lead
Partners: IUSTI, SAFRAN, Pprime

Future Research Directions

Looking ahead, my research will continue to push the boundaries of fluid mechanics and machine learning integration, with a particular focus on developing innovative approaches for flow control and prediction in complex engineering systems.

The following directions represent the core focus of my upcoming research initiatives, aiming to address critical challenges in energy efficiency, thermal management, and sustainable engineering.

Multi-scale Control of Wall Turbulence

Development of innovative control strategies operating simultaneously at different scales of turbulence, allowing for enhancement or limitation of heat transfer across a wide range of Reynolds numbers. These advances will have significant impact on renewable energy development and improvement of existing industrial processes using heat exchangers.

Hybrid Data-Physics Framework

Development of a methodological framework integrating physical knowledge and data-driven approaches to design optimal active control strategies. This approach will offer new possibilities for improving industrial processes, especially relevant with the recent development of additive printing that removes constraints related to complex geometries.

Adaptive Modeling for Thermofluid Systems

Design of models capable of adapting to varying flow conditions, allowing for accurate prediction and robust control in variable industrial environments. These adaptive models will pave the way for intelligent control systems to optimize heat exchanger performance in real-time.
